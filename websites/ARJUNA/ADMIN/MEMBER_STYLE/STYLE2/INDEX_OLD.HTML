David B. Ingham

Biography

David Ingham received his B.Eng in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Northumbria University in 1991 and an M.Sc. in Computing Software and Systems Design from Newcastle University in 1992. He is currently a Research Associate in the Department of Computing Science at Newcastle, where he is a member of the team developing the Arjuna reliable distributed programming system. His research interests include distributed computing, reliable systems, object-oriented computing and distributed-debugging tools. His recent work has been in the area of Web engineering, in particular bringing object-oriented technology and distributed systems research concepts to the web.

Current Activities

  • Dependable Web Services - Research sponsored by Hewlett-Packard Labs (Bristol) focusing on bringing distributed object technology to the Web, concentrating in particular on dependability aspects.
  • W3Objects - Research aimed at developing an extensible, object-oriented architecture for world-wide web services.
  • Arjuna - responsibility for the crash recovery sub-system of the Arjuna reliable distributed programming environment.
