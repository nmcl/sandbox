Summary

Explosive growth in the use of tools and services for searching and accessing information available over the Internet coupled with advances in communications technology are opening up a wide class of new applications ranging from home entertainment to electronic commerce. Thus, large-scale distributed computing systems (LSDCS) comprising several thousands to millions of individual computer systems (nodes) are rapidly coming into existence.

The main objective of the Working Group (WG) is to create a forum where principles and practice of designing and implementing LSDCS can be discussed and new ideas and practical experience shared. The WG activities will focus on research issues concerned with the development of software technology for the creation of network services for information sharing. The WG will run for three years starting from May 1996.

All the academic members of the Broadcast-WG have worked together on Project 6360 on LSDCS, called Broadcast (which terminated in August 1995) and have built up good inter-site links for collaboration. Research groups from EPFL (led by Prof. André Schiper), Univ. Bologna (led by Prof. Ozalp Babaoglu) and IRISA (led by Prof. Michel Raynal) have made notable contributions to the theory of distributed systems. Research groups from INESC (led by Prof. Paulo Veríssimo), INRIA (led by Dr. Marc Shapiro), IRISA (led by Dr. Michel Banâtre), IMAG (led by Prof. Sacha Krakowiak) and Univ. Newcastle (led by Prof. Santosh Shrivastava) have contributed to the practice of designing and implementing distributed systems. Researchers from ANSA Laboratory (led by Dr. Andrew Herbert, Chief Architect) have played influential roles in bringing distributed object technology to industry and contributing to the development of standards (through their involvement with OMG, TINA etc.). Researchers from Chorus Systèmes (led by Dr. Michel Gien, technical director), are one of the worlds leading suppliers of microkernel technology. The Consortium as a whole thus represents a group of researchers active in both the theory and practice of distributed systems; this is its major strength.

Closed workshop will be organised around a small set of topics. Industrial partners of the WG will provide the industrial perspective on the research work and its relevance to specific application requirements. Workshops will contain application specific sessions, such as: fault tolerance in telecommunication systems, bringing distributed object technology to the World Wide Web, and microkernel support for responsive services. Open workshops will also be organised to act as a forum for presenting the latest research results, technological developments and trends in the area of LSDCS. Invitations to the open workshop will be sent to the scientific community (specially to members of related projects and the Research Network on Distributed Computing Architectures, CaberNet), to industrials groups, and to the EU. The WG will also organise European Research Seminar on Advances in Distributed Systems (ERSADS). This Seminar will combine an Advanced School and a Research Workshop, and is intended to be a major European forum for interchange between students, junior researchers, and professionals in the area of Distributed Systems. The 2nd ERSADS was held in March 1997 in Zinal, Switzerland.
