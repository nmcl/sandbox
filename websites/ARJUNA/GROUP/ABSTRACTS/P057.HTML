Fault-Tolerant Execution of Computationally and Storage Intensive Parallel Programs Over a Network of Workstations: a case study

J.A. Smith and S.K. Shrivastava

Abstract: We consider the issues affecting the speedup attainable for computations that are demanding in both storage and computing requirements, e.g. several hundred megabytes of data and hours of computation time. Specifically, we investigate the performance of matrix multiplication. We have implemented a fault-tolerant system for the bag of tasks computation structure using atomic actions (equivalent here to atomic transactions) to operate on persistent objects, encapsulated in C++ classes. We report on the experimental results obtained. Our analysis, backed up by the experimental results, shows how best to structure such computations for obtaining reasonable speedups.
