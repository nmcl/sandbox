Marinho Barcellos

Biography

Marinho Barcellos received his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees, both in Computing Science, from UFRGS-Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul, Porto Alegre, Brazil (1986-89 and 1990-93, respectively). Between April 1993 and October 1994 he worked as a Research Assistant at that institution, coordinating the development of a platform for parallel processing through clusters of networked RISC machines. In the period 1990-94 he has lectured on Data Structures, Operating Systems, and Distributed Systems, at universities UCS, ULBRA, and UNISINOS, being at present on leave from the latter. Current research work, under a Ph.D. sponsored by CAPES, is related to issues in large-scale multicast communication, as error control, recovery, flow control, congestion control, and in particular, implosion control.
