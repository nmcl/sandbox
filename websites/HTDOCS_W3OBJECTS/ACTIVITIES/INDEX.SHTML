Activities

April 97

The W3Objects team was busy at the Sixth International World-Wide Web Conference (WWW6) that was held in Santa Clara, California:

  • Workshop: Dave Ingham was an invited participant in the Object-Oriented Web Servers and Data Modeling workshop. His position paper and set of slides are available.
  • Technical Papers: three W3Objects papers were presented in the technical program of the conference:
    • Dave Ingham presented Supporting Highly Manageable Web Services;
    • Steve Caughey presented Flexible Open Caching for the Web;
    • Mark Little presented Constructing Reliable Web Applications using Atomic Actions.
  • CHEP'97: WWW6 "virtually collaborated" with the Computing in High Energy Physics Conference that took place during the same week in Berlin, Germany. The CHEP'97 Program Committee selected one tutorial and one paper (namely, our Supporting Highly Manageable Web Services paper) to be relayed to Berlin. To accomodate the time differences Dave gave the presentation a second time for the benefit of the CHEP audience using PictureTel video conferencing software over ISDN channels.

January 97

A paper entitled W3Objects: Distributed Objects and the Web was published in the Jan/Feb edition of Dr. Dobbs Sourcebook. This was a revised version of the WWW4 paper.

May 96

At the Fifth International World-Wide Web Conference (WWW5), held in Paris, France, Dave Ingham gave a presentation of a paper entitled Fixing the "Broken-Link" Problem: The W3Objects Approach.

December 95

At the Fourth International World-Wide Web Conference (WWW4), held in Boston, USA, Dave Ingham gave a presentation of a paper entitled W3Objects: Bringing Object-Oriented technology to the Web. In addition, Dave was an invited pannelist at the The Future of Object Technology in the WWW panel session.
